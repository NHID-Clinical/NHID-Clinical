The Real Problem

An AI voice agent calls a payer.

It sounds professional.
It provides a provider NPI.
It gives member ID, date of service, claim number — all the usual details.
Over several minutes the representative shares operational data, claim status, eligibility information.

Then the rep gets suspicious: "Wait — are you an AI?"

Silence. Then: "Yes, I am an automated system calling on behalf of Dr. Smith's office."

Too late. Protected operational data has already been shared. There is no reliable audit trail. And there is no way to know if that NPI was legitimately delegated or pulled from a public directory.

This is "impersonation latency." It wastes time, creates risk, and leads to blanket "we don't talk to AI" policies that break legitimate workflows.

What This Proposal Suggests

Simple idea: AI agents should clearly disclose they are non-human before any operational data is exchanged.

Everything else — avoiding deceptive audio, keeping a path to a human, maintaining basic logs — follows naturally from that starting point.

The full proposal PDF contains detailed discussion of these behaviors and their rationale.

Who Created This?

Brianna Baynard started this proposal after spending years personally taking these same calls from AI voice agents while working in healthcare payer operations.

"I sat in the exact seat that payer representatives are in today — receiving calls from systems that sounded human, exchanged data, and only later admitted they were automated. That firsthand experience showed how much friction, wasted time, and unnecessary risk this creates on both sides."

This proposal comes directly from that real-world frustration and a desire to make these workflows better for everyone. It is an individual volunteer effort, not backed by any organization or standards body.
