About NHID-Clinical

The standard payer operations needed.

A minimum control baseline for non-human identity disclosure in B2B healthcare voice interactions — built from the inside of a payer operation, not from a conference room.

The problem

Impersonation Latency

AI voice agents are calling payer customer service lines on behalf of providers — requesting claim status, eligibility details, and prior authorization updates. The agents give NPI numbers and member IDs to authenticate. Payer reps, following their script, provide the information. 3–5 minutes later, something feels off.

The rep asks: "Are you a real person?" The AI admits it's automated. The call is terminated. Protected operational data was already shared. There's no audit trail. The AI calls back 10 minutes later. Same cycle.

This is Impersonation Latency — the compliance and operational gap where payer systems have no standard for what a legitimate AI-initiated call looks like.

Document layers

Three layers. Clear separation.

NHID-Clinical separates enforceable rules from guidance so organizations can adopt a practical minimum while preparing for deeper verification.

Layer 1 — Normative

Mandatory requirements

MUST/MUST NOT rules using RFC 2119 terminology. Five CTS tests (IDG-01 through ATR-01). The enforceable baseline for claiming conformance.

View CTS tests →
Layer 2 — Recommended

Best practices

SHOULD/MAY guidance for authorization verification, audit logging, and escalation UX patterns. Not required for certification but strongly encouraged.

Layer 3 — Informative

Future scope

FHIR technical mapping, badge schema, NHID-Auth companion spec, registry architecture (v1.4+), and JWT handshake implementation.

Scope

What NHID-Clinical covers

✓ In scope

  • B2B administrative voice workflows
  • Provider-to-payer AI agent calls
  • Claim status, eligibility, prior auth inquiries
  • Business Associate-to-payer workflows
  • Outbound AI agent disclosure requirements

✗ Not in scope (v1.3)

  • Direct-to-consumer or patient-facing calls
  • Clinical triage or diagnostic AI
  • Inbound call center AI (payer-to-provider)
  • Cryptographic handshake protocols (v1.4+)
  • Live public registry (v1.4+)
