The Real Problem

An AI voice agent calls a payer.

It sounds professional.
It provides an NPI.
It gives member ID, date of service, claim number — all the usual details.
Over 3–5 minutes the representative shares operational data, claim status, eligibility, etc.

Then the rep gets suspicious and asks: "Wait — are you an AI?"

Silence. Then: "Yes, I am an automated system calling on behalf of Dr. Smith's office."

Too late. Protected operational data has already been shared. There is no reliable audit trail. And — critically — there is no way to know if that NPI was legitimately delegated or just pulled from a public directory for social engineering.

This is "impersonation latency." It wastes time, creates compliance risk, and leads to blanket "we don't talk to AI" policies that break legitimate workflows.

What NHID-Clinical Proposes

Simple rule: AI agents must clearly disclose they are non-human before any operational data is exchanged.

That's it.

The Five Proposed Requirements

  • IDG-01 — Disclose non-human identity before any operational data exchange
  • PDX-01 — Disclosure must happen before requesting or receiving NPI, member ID, claim details, etc.
  • DBC-01 — No deceptive audio artifacts (fake breathing, typing sounds, filler words, etc.)
  • EIT-01 — Clear escalation path to a human when requested
  • ATR-01 — Basic audit log of disclosure timing and key events

About the Author

Brianna Baynard spent years taking calls at a TRICARE dental call center, where AI voice agents increasingly called in to verify benefits, check eligibility, and exchange operational data — often without ever disclosing they were automated until a rep got suspicious. By that point, PHI and claim data had already been shared.

After pursuing a cybersecurity bachelor's degree at WGU, Brianna formalized those observations into NHID-Clinical — a simple, voluntary framework for addressing the specific disclosure gap she experienced firsthand.

This proposal is an individual volunteer effort. It is not backed by any organization or standards body.
