NHID-Clinical

An open proposal for transparent AI voice agents in healthcare payer–provider calls.

NHID-Clinical proposes that AI voice agents clearly identify themselves as non-human before exchanging operational data in B2B healthcare workflows — claims, eligibility, prior auth, and similar interactions.

Initiated by Brianna Baynard. This is not an official standard, accredited certification body, or regulatory requirement. It is a voluntary open proposal open for feedback and real-world testing.

NIST AI Safety Institute Submission

NHID-Clinical Submitted as a Public Comment to NIST

NHID-Clinical was submitted as a public comment to NIST docket NIST-2025-0035 (AI-agent security), proposing a voluntary governance framework for healthcare voice AI disclosure. This is a voluntary industry proposal designed to complement — not replace — existing rules around HIPAA, TCPA, and emerging AI transparency guidelines.

NIST Docket: NIST-2025-0035-0026  ·  Submitted: January 12, 2026

The proposal

A shared framework for transparent voice AI workflows

NHID-Clinical proposes behavioral requirements for AI voice agents in healthcare — disclosure timing, authorization verification, and escalation protocols. The goal is a shared vocabulary that payers, providers, and platforms can reference when evaluating AI caller behavior.

What problem does this solve?

Reduce impersonation latency before sensitive workflow data is exchanged.

NHID-Clinical proposes a minimum baseline for AI voice agents in B2B healthcare administrative workflows. This proposal gives payers, providers, and platforms a shared way to disclose automation, verify authorization, and escalate to a human when needed.
