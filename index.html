An open governance proposal

NHID-Clinical: A voluntary disclosure framework for AI voice agents in provider-to-payer calls

NHID-Clinical proposes that AI callers should identify themselves, prove authorization, and leave an auditable trail before exchanging patient data. Initiated by Brianna Baynard. Not an official standard or regulatory requirement.

View the Proposal →

Open source specification seeking feedback and adoption from healthcare platforms and payers.

NIST

Regulatory context

Submitted as public comment to NIST docket NIST-2025-0035 (AI-agent security).

NHID-Clinical was submitted as a public comment to the National Institute of Standards and Technology's AI governance initiative. The proposal addresses impersonation latency through pre-data-exchange disclosure controls for AI voice agents in B2B payer–provider workflows.

NIST Docket: NIST-2025-0035-0026  ·  Submitted: January 12, 2026

Expected Impact

What changes when AI callers disclose upfront.

A mid-sized payer processing 5,000 AI voice calls per month could see measurable operational improvements within the first 90 days of adoption.

Green Lane Benefit: Compliant calls get faster handling by providers who trust transparent workflows — bypassing the "are you human?" challenge cycle entirely.

Projections based on industry admin waste benchmarks. Pilot data available under NDA.

The governance layer

Governance Infrastructure for Voice AI Platforms

NHID-Clinical proposes behavioral requirements for healthcare voice agents — disclosure timing, authorization verification, and escalation protocols. Voice platforms provide the engine. NHID-Clinical provides a proposed safety layer. Platforms that adopt NHID-Clinical can demonstrate transparent, auditable workflows that payers and providers can evaluate.

What problem does this solve?

Reduce impersonation latency before sensitive workflow data is exchanged.

NHID-Clinical proposes a minimum control baseline for AI voice agents in B2B healthcare administrative workflows. The proposal gives payers, providers, and platforms a shared framework to disclose automation, verify authorization, and escalate to a human when needed.

AI voice call enters the workflow
Disclosure happens too late or only after challenge
NHID-Clinical moves identity disclosure before data exchange

The green lane principle

A simple path for transparent AI voice workflows.

When AI agents identify themselves upfront and follow this framework, providers save time, payers reduce operational burden, and both sides gain clarity.

01

Disclose before data exchange

AI callers identify themselves clearly before operational or protected workflow details are shared.

02

Verify authorization

Payers can distinguish validated AI agents from spoofed or undisclosed automation.

03

Escalate safely

When a human representative is required, the path out is documented and auditable.
