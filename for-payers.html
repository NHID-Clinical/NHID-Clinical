For Payers & Procurement Teams

Copy-paste NHID-Clinical compliance language for your vendor contracts and RFPs. Require AI voice agents to disclose themselves, escalate safely, and maintain audit trails.

RFP Language

Vendor AI Voice Agent Compliance Requirements

Use the language below in your RFPs, vendor contracts, and procurement documents. Vendors implementing NHID-Clinical can request implementation review at help@nhid-clinical.org.

VENDOR AI VOICE AGENT COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

Vendor must demonstrate compliance with NHID-Clinical v1.3 baseline 
requirements, including:

1. Proactive non-human identity disclosure prior to any PHI or 
   operational data exchange (IDG-01)

2. Prohibition of deceptive behavioral artifacts in voice synthesis 
   (DBC-01)

3. Cryptographic audit trails with timestamp verification (ATR-01)

4. Pre-data exchange authorization gates (PDX-01)

5. Immediate escalation to human representatives upon request (EIT-01)

Vendor must provide certification documentation (Level 1 minimum, 
Level 2 preferred) from nhid-clinical.org demonstrating conformance 
testing results.

Vendor must maintain audit-ready transaction logs showing:
- Timestamp of non-human identity disclosure
- Timestamp of first operational or PHI data request
- Verification that disclosure preceded data request
- Escalation handling and reference ID issuance

Non-compliance with NHID-Clinical baseline requirements constitutes 
a material breach of this agreement.

Why Require NHID-Clinical?

Business Case for Payers

Operational Efficiency

Upfront AI disclosure eliminates 10–15 seconds of "are you human?" verification per call. For a payer processing 5,000 AI calls per month, that's 120+ staff hours saved.

Regulatory Alignment

NHID-Clinical aligns with emerging state AI disclosure laws (CA, TX) and NIST AI Risk Management Framework guidance. Demonstrate proactive compliance to regulators.

Audit Trail & Compliance

Cryptographic logging of disclosure, data exchange, and escalation events creates an immutable record for regulatory review and internal compliance audits.

Vendor Accountability

NHID-Clinical conformance testing provides clear pass/fail criteria. No ambiguity. Vendors either meet the standard or they don't.

Implementation Guidance

How to Use NHID-Clinical in Your Procurement

Step 1: Add to RFP

Include the compliance language above in your RFP requirements section.

Step 2: Vendor Response

Request vendors provide evidence of NHID-Clinical conformance testing results for all five requirements (IDG-01, PDX-01, DBC-01, EIT-01, ATR-01).

Step 3: Implementation Review

Vendors can request implementation review from NHID-Clinical at help@nhid-clinical.org. Official certification program launches Q3 2026.

Step 4: Contract Language

Include audit trail and escalation requirements in your service level agreements (SLAs). Require vendors to maintain NHID-Clinical conformance throughout the contract term.
