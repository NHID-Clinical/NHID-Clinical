Optional Reference Language

For Payers & Procurement Teams

Optional reference language you can adapt when asking vendors about AI voice agent transparency. NHID-Clinical is a voluntary open proposal — not a formal standard or regulatory requirement. Consult your legal team before using any language in binding contracts.

Important: This is not a formal standard, certification, or regulatory requirement. NHID-Clinical is an early open proposal by Brianna Baynard. The language below is optional and illustrative only. Your organization should make its own determination about appropriate vendor requirements.

Optional RFP Language

Sample Vendor AI Voice Agent Transparency Language

The following is example language you may optionally reference or adapt. It is not a certification or audit requirement.

OPTIONAL VENDOR AI VOICE AGENT TRANSPARENCY LANGUAGE
(Reference only — not a formal standard or requirement)

Vendor is encouraged to demonstrate alignment with NHID-Clinical v1.3,
a voluntary open proposal for AI voice agent transparency, including:

1. Proactive non-human identity disclosure prior to any PHI or
   operational data exchange (IDG-01)

2. No deceptive behavioral artifacts in voice synthesis (DBC-01)

3. Basic audit logging of disclosure timing and key events (ATR-01)

4. No operational data exchange before disclosure (PDX-01)

5. Clear escalation to a human representative upon request (EIT-01)

Vendor may optionally provide self-testing documentation or peer
feedback results from nhid-clinical.org.

Note: NHID-Clinical is a voluntary open proposal by Brianna Baynard —
not an official standard. Consult your legal team before including
any binding language in contracts.

Why Some Payers Reference This

The Problem This Addresses

AI voice agents calling payer staff often sound human and exchange operational data before ever disclosing they are automated. This wastes time, creates compliance risk, and leads to blanket "no AI" policies that break legitimate workflows.

The five proposed requirements in NHID-Clinical are simple: disclose early, no fake human sounds, allow escalation, keep basic logs. Some payers find it useful to reference this language when evaluating vendors, even though it is not a formal standard.
