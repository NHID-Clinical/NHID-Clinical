Open Proposal: Non-Human Identity Disclosure for Healthcare Voice Workflows

NIST Public Comment: NIST-2025-0035-0026

May 2026 — Open Proposal Status: This is a voluntary open proposal initiated by Brianna Baynard. It is not an official standard, accredited body, or regulatory requirement. It is shared for feedback and real-world testing.

Abstract

NHID-Clinical proposes simple behavioral rules for AI voice agents in payer–provider administrative calls so humans immediately know they are speaking with automation.

Problem

AI agents often hide that they are automated until after sharing operational data. This creates "impersonation latency" — wasted time, trust issues, and security risks.

Proposed Requirements

IDG-01 – Identity Disclosure Gate

Disclose non-human identity before any operational data exchange.

PDX-01 – Pre-Data Exchange Disclosure

Disclosure must occur before requesting or receiving protected data.

DBC-01 – No Deceptive Artifacts

No simulated breathing, typing sounds, filler words, or other deceptive artifacts.

EIT-01 – Escalation Path

Provide a clear escalation path to a human when requested.

ATR-01 – Audit Trail

Maintain basic audit logs (disclosure time, data request time, call outcome).

Implementation Guidance

  1. Audit current call disclosure timing
  2. Remove fake human audio artifacts
  3. Add reliable escalation (reference + callback)
  4. Log key events
  5. Test and share feedback
