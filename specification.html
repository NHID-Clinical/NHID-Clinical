Open Proposal: Non-Human Identity Disclosure for Healthcare Voice Workflows

NIST Public Comment: NIST-2025-0035-0026

May 2026 — Open Proposal Status: This is a voluntary open proposal initiated by Brianna Baynard. It is not an official standard, accredited body, or regulatory requirement. It is shared for feedback and real-world testing.

Abstract

NHID-Clinical proposes simple behavioral rules for AI voice agents in payer–provider administrative calls so humans immediately know they are speaking with automation.

Problem

AI agents often hide that they are automated until after sharing operational data (NPI, member ID, claim numbers, etc.). This creates "impersonation latency" — wasted time, trust issues, and security risks.

Proposed Requirements

IDG-01 – Identity Disclosure Gate

Disclose non-human identity before soliciting or receiving any operational data.

PDX-01 – Pre-Data Exchange Disclosure

Disclosure must happen before any protected operational data is requested or shared.

DBC-01 – No Deceptive Artifacts

Do not use simulated breathing, typing sounds, filler words, or other tricks designed to sound human.

EIT-01 – Escalation Path

Provide a clear way to reach a human representative when requested or when the AI cannot proceed.

ATR-01 – Audit Trail

Keep basic logs showing disclosure time, first data request time, call outcome, and escalation reference (if any).

Implementation Guidance

  1. Check your current calls for when disclosure happens
  2. Remove fake human sounds from your voice system
  3. Build a simple escalation path (reference number + callback)
  4. Log the key events
  5. Test it yourself and share feedback
