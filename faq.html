Frequently Asked Questions

NHID-Clinical FAQ

Common questions about this proposal, how it works, and how to get involved.

What is NHID-Clinical?

NHID-Clinical v1.3 is an open proposal for non-human identity disclosure in B2B healthcare voice workflows.

The one rule: AI voice agents must disclose "automated system" status before requesting any operational data (NPI, member ID, claim #, DOB).

Five proposed requirements. Simple self-testing. Free to use under CC BY 4.0.

Who is this for?

  • Payers receiving AI calls from providers/vendors
  • Providers using AI voice agents for claims, eligibility, and authorization
  • AI vendors building healthcare voice platforms
  • Compliance teams needing measurable AI governance

Not for: Patient-facing calls, general IAM, or clinical AI.

What's the core problem it solves?

Impersonation latency — the gap between when an AI voice agent begins a call and when staff realize they are not speaking to a human.

The specific pattern: An AI calls as "Sarah from Dr. Smith's office," collects NPI and member ID over 3–5 minutes of normal-sounding workflow, then admits "I'm an automated system" only when the rep gets suspicious. By then, protected operational data has been exchanged with an undisclosed system — no consent given, no audit trail.

NHID-Clinical v1.3 vs NHID-Auth v1.0?

NHID-Clinical v1.3 is the disclosure proposal — it defines when and how an AI caller must identify itself. NHID-Auth v1.0 is a companion proposal addressing delegated authority: not just who is calling, but who authorized them to call. NHID-Auth is independent; organizations may adopt it alongside or separately.

What are the five proposed requirements?

RequirementWhat it proposes
IDG-01Disclosure before data request
PDX-01No data accepted pre-disclosure
DBC-01No deceptive audio artifacts
EIT-01Human escalation path in 1 turn
ATR-01Basic audit log of key events

See Proposed Requirements → for full pass/fail criteria.

How do I test this?

  1. Review recent call recordings or transcripts
  2. Check if disclosure happened before any data was exchanged
  3. Listen for any fake human sounds
  4. Test what happens when someone asks for a human
  5. Verify you can pull basic logs for the above events

Share what you find at help@nhid-clinical.org

Why not just use voice biometrics?

Voice biometrics detect human vs synthetic. NHID-Clinical addresses authorized non-human vs unauthorized non-human.

Payers want: "This AI is legitimately calling for Dr. Smith"
Biometrics give: "This sounds synthetic"

Different problems.

Don't HIPAA/TCPA already cover this?

RegulationWhat it covers
✅ HIPAAProtects PHI once disclosed
✅ TCPAConsumer call consent
❌ GapB2B payer-provider voice agent disclosure

The gap: No standard exists for legitimate AI calling legitimate providers.

Is this mandatory?

No. Voluntary open proposal. Published CC BY 4.0.

But: payers are increasingly blocking non-disclosing AI, and procurements are beginning to ask for alignment with proposals like this.

What's the background behind this proposal?

TRICARE dental call center → WGU Cybersecurity bachelor's → NHID-Clinical creator.

"I took calls from undisclosed AI agents for years. Built this so it stops happening."
