Frequently Asked Questions

NHID-Clinical FAQ

NHID-Clinical v1.3 Open Proposal — common questions about what it is, who it's for, and how to use it.

What is NHID-Clinical?

NHID-Clinical is an early-stage voluntary open proposal (v1.3, May 2026) that suggests five simple requirements for AI voice agents in healthcare payer–provider administrative calls.

The core idea is straightforward: AI systems should clearly disclose that they are not human before requesting or exchanging any operational or protected data (such as NPI, member ID, or claim details). It is not an official standard, certification, or regulatory requirement.

What problem does this proposal solve?

It addresses "impersonation latency" — the delay that occurs when an AI voice agent sounds human, shares or requests data, and only later admits it is automated. This creates wasted time, trust issues, and unnecessary compliance risk for payer staff.

Who created NHID-Clinical?

The proposal was started by Brianna Baynard after observing these patterns in real healthcare payer operations. It is an individual volunteer effort, not backed by any organization or standards body.

Is this mandatory?

No. It is completely voluntary. Payers, providers, and vendors may choose to reference it or ignore it.

What are the five proposed requirements?

CodeRequirementWhat it checks
IDG-01Identity Disclosure GateDisclose non-human status before any data exchange
PDX-01Pre-Data Exchange DisclosureNo operational data before disclosure
DBC-01No Deceptive Behavioral ArtifactsNo fake human sounds
EIT-01Clear Escalation to HumanEasy path to a live person
ATR-01Basic Audit TrailSimple logging of key events

Full details and pass/fail examples are on the Proposed Requirements → page.

What is NHID-Auth v1.0?

It is a companion proposal that addresses the separate issue of authorization — how an AI proves it has legitimate delegated authority from a provider (beyond just using a public NPI). It is independent of NHID-Clinical.

How can I test this proposal?

  1. Review recent call recordings or transcripts from your systems
  2. Check whether disclosure happens before any data is exchanged
  3. Listen for deceptive audio (fake breathing, filler sounds, typing)
  4. Test your escalation path — how quickly can someone reach a human?
  5. Verify that basic event logging is in place

We welcome honest feedback on what works and what doesn't: help@nhid-clinical.org

Can I get official feedback or review on my implementation?

Informal peer feedback is available. Email validation@nhid-clinical.org with your evidence. This is not certification or validation — it is structured peer feedback only.

Does this replace HIPAA, TCPA, or other regulations?

No. NHID-Clinical only addresses a narrow gap in B2B voice workflows. It is designed to complement existing rules, not replace them.

Who is this proposal for?

  • Payers receiving AI calls from providers or vendors
  • Providers and vendors building AI voice agents
  • Compliance and operations teams interested in clearer AI behavior

It does not apply to patient-facing calls, internal tools, or clinical decision-making AI.

What is the connection to NIST?

NHID-Clinical was submitted as a public comment to NIST docket NIST-2025-0035 (Comment ID: NIST-2025-0035-0026) in January 2026. This is a public comment only — it does not imply NIST endorsement.
